Instructions

In this experiment, we'll be playing a communication game. The game has two roles, which will be assigned randomly. Since you will not know which role you will play until the game starts, you will be responsible for understanding the responsibilities and goals of both roles. If you are the first person to arrive in a game, you must wait shortly for another player to join. Once both players have joined the game, you will see a display like the following:

On the right side of the screen, labeled "Play-space", you will see a grid filled with various objects. On the left side of the screen, labeled "Chatbox", you will see a chat window that you can use to communicate with the other player.

If you are the "director", the grid will be marked to indicate how the objects inside are to be moved. You'll have to give instructions to the other player telling them how to move the objects. You are responsible for instructing to move the items so that they reach the final positions that are shown in the picture. Be sure to give your instructions as clearly as you possibly could. Given the below screen, for example, you would want to tell the other player to move the umbrella to the right.

If you are the "matcher", your job will be to follow the director's instructions as best as you can. You will follow these instructions by clicking and dragging items from one cell of the grid to another. To understand your behavior, we will be recording the location of your mouse within the grid. For this reason, we will ask you to click on a circle in the center of the grid between instructions or if you make an incorrect move. This screen will look like the following:

We will also be recording the messages both of you send.

An important point for both players to keep in mind is that some cells are occluded . When the background of a cell is white, the director will not know what is behind it . If you are the matcher, you must pay close attention to the fact that the director will not be able to see the items in the occluded slots.

Before we start, there are 3 more things we'd like you to keep in mind.

  1. During each round, please talk only about the game. Try not to talk about the objects except as needed to move them around the grid.
  2. About half of the director's instructions will be scripted. They will appear in the chat box and must be sent unaltered.
  3. If you are the matcher, you will click to put a certain object from each grid into a brown bag, and then, with the item still in the bag, put it back in the slot where it belongs.
There will be 8 different grids with different items.
